The Feasibility Study Option for Significant Aspects and Impacts

Significant aspects and impacts identified earlier should be considered a priority. Often solutions are found, but when there is no visible solution, it can be addressed by creating a feasibility study. The feasibility study demonstrates that the significant impact is not ignored.

Example: Feasibility study to search for a VOC free paint that is as effective as the current paint

Specific: Search for and test VOC free paints on panel surfaces
Measurable: Research available suppliers of paints data base by April 30
Actionable: Purchase 10 gallons new VOC free paint
Test on 10 panels/do 5 quality control tests by August 31
Realistic: Recommended by paint manufacturers based on prior testing
Time based: Decision re purchasing new paint to be made by October 1st



When setting objectives, take into account lifecycle considerations, such as when buying raw materials or disposal at end of life, including:

  • What is under the direct control of the business
  • What the business can influence indirectly

Examples include reducing, reusing or recycling. The choice of objectives can come from many sources such as observation, suggestions, new knowledge, creative ideas.

Think about SMART when documenting your objectives: Specific, Measurable, Actionable, Realistic, Time-based.

Choose carefully and avoid over promising. It is better to have a shorter realistic list of objectives than under-delivering on a longer list (greenwashing).

POLICY

Your environmental policy is a statement of the overall environmental direction of your business. It is a declaration of your commitment to the environment. Post it publicly, in locations of your choice such as business office, website, social media platform, etc.
The policy can be short or long, and the text is your choice, but it must include these commitments:

  1. compliance with all applicable environmental laws and regulations
  2. conformance with the ISO 14001:2015 Environmental Management Standard
  3. continual improvement

Each year, depending on the new annual objectives chosen, the policy should indicate the new environmental direction of the business. Examples: reducing (further) energy use, purchasing (more) green materials, doing more training. There is no requirement to disclose specific objectives. The policy is easiest to formulate after the creation of the environmental objectives.



An example of a simple company policy statement:

Company name Environmental Policy
Company name recognizes its responsibility to protect the environment as a guiding principle and a key component of its business performance. We are committed to integrate the consideration of the environment in all our decision making and activities.
Company name complies with all environmental laws and regulations affecting its operations, conforms to and follows the ISO 14001:2015 Environmental Standard, and commits to continually improve.
Company name current objectives are to further reduce energy use, increase the efficient use of resources and materials, and extend environmental training to employees.
Company name aims to contribute in a meaningful way to the achievement of reductions in carbon emissions to address the current global climate challenge.

NONCONFORMITIES

Nonconformities are incidents when actions and procedures put in place to achieve environmental objectives are not followed. Mistakes should be rare, but can happen, and ideally should not be repeated. When a nonconformity occurs, it requires corrective action, the steps you take to fix the problem of nonconformity:

  • first, to describe what exactly happened
  • next to find out why it happened
  • then to create and put in place a solution that will avoid it from happening in the future
  • finally, to document the process. Nonconformities are important learning during audits when evaluating compliance with objectives. This is required to remain in compliance.

Example of a documented nonconformity:

Procedure: Do not idle electrical saw between batches
Error details (what, when, where, who):
Electrical saw operator idled equipment between batches on second shift (date)
Root Cause (why):
- New temporary employee, did not receive proper procedure training when started work
- There is no training procedure in place for new temporary employees
Corrective Action:
Include training procedure for new temporary employees as a step in the intake process and
Correction Completed:
Done, communicated and documented within two/three weeks from non-compliance (dates)

Discuss, fix, train, communicate, document and file every time an action or a procedure to achieve an objective is not followed.

AUDITS AND MANAGEMENT REVIEW

Auditing is a key part of the ISO 14001:2015 System. Its function is to evaluate performance against the objectives that were chosen at the beginning of your annual cycle and conformance with compliance obligations.
The major audit is at the end of the year when all objectives are to be fully completed.
To ensure compliance, periodic audits - at the end of every quarter - are undertaken during the year, to look at progress in achieving objectives.
The auditors are people working in the business, and no special auditing qualifications are necessary. Ideally another person than the one responsible for the objective should do the audit for the objective being evaluated. Auditors can be different people at different checkpoints.
As part of your annual subscription, greenmark will review your audit results.
Note: the ISO 14001:2015 Standard does not require, nor does it favour the option of using official registrars over the options of self-declaration or other third party.

Management Review

This review should be completed at the end of the year: can be during or right after the fourth Quarter Audit or soon after. The Key Decision Maker/s and the Environment Manager do the following:

  1. Evaluate the overall environmental performance in light of the policy and commitment to objectives
  2. Identify and commit to new objectives taking into account changes in the business during the last year
  3. Sign off on starting a new ISO 14001:2015 cycle and celebrate success
